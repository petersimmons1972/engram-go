## SOTA Bar

From `docs/lme-benchmark-learnings.md §External Research` and external sources referenced therein.

| System | LME Score | Architecture |
|---|---|---|
| **OMEGA** | **~95.4%** | Not detailed in our sources — cited as SOTA. |
| **Mem0** | **~94.4%** | Not detailed in our sources — cited as near-SOTA. |
| **Mastra observational-memory** | **~95%** | "Observational memory" — stores structured observations from conversations at write time rather than raw chunks; retrieval operates on distilled facts. |
| **Engram (current best)** | **~42.6%** | Vector-RAG (raw sessions) + Sonnet generator + preference-enumerate + enumerate-first + context-topk 30. |

**Gap:** ~52–53pp between our current best and SOTA.

**Key architectural observations from our experiments:**
1. SOTA systems appear to use **write-time extraction / structured memory** — transforming raw session content into structured facts/preferences/events at ingest, not at retrieval time. This is the "observational memory" pattern.
2. Our diagnostic (retrieval is ~100% recall, generation is the gap) points in the same direction: the RAG loop surfaces raw session text; the generator must extract, scope, dedup, and aggregate on the fly under noise. SOTA systems pre-do this work.
3. The `single-session-preference` type (our 30%, SOTA presumably near 90%+) is the clearest signal: engram returns raw conversation chunks; SOTA systems likely index extracted preferences as first-class objects at write time.
4. The deterministic-aggregation experiment confirmed the inverse: extracted events alone (without the raw session fallback) cap at ~17%. The right architecture is **structured atoms as index into raw**, not atoms as replacement.

## Rebuild-vs-Fix Candidates

### KEEP (foundational / ceiling-opening)

| Lever | Why Keep |
|---|---|
| **Sonnet (Claude) generator** | +8pp single largest lever; generation quality is the dominant gap. In prod this is the client model — already the architecture. |
| **preference-enumerate synthesis directive** | +10pp strict-correct on preference; ships as client-side directive (engram-go#1113). Low risk, high value. |
| **enumerate-first + context-topk 30 (coverage+synthesis)** | +6.7pp, paired-flip confirmed, the banked win. context-topk 30 is the coverage mechanism; enumerate-first is the synthesis mechanism. Together they are foundational. |
| **Write-time structured extraction (not yet built)** | The SOTA gap (52pp) strongly suggests this is the missing architecture layer. All three SOTA systems use write-time distillation. The preference case is cleanest: extract preference objects at ingest, index them as first-class items. Exploratory — not yet an experiment, but the single highest-probability path to 95%. |
| **events-as-INDEX (semantic variant, not yet run)** | Oracle atom+raw shows 60% ceiling; lexical variant is a lower bound. Semantic normalization of extracted objects is the untested path to making the index signal discriminative. Depends on events-index-experiment result. |

## COMMANDER'S ADJUDICATION (Eisenhower, 2026-06-22) — two corrections that change the strategic conclusion

**Correction 1 — the "52pp gap" is NOT apples-to-apples; the real gap is UNKNOWN.** Every engram number in this registry (35.6% → 42.6%) is on the **hard subset** (LME-M / v9-failures-135). The SOTA 95% figures are on **LongMemEval-S** (the published 500-question standard set), which we have **never run** (FINDINGS.md says so explicitly). Comparing our hard-subset score to SOTA's standard-set score is unsound. **The single highest-priority open row is "LME-S baseline — NOT YET MEASURED."** Until it exists, the fix-vs-rebuild *magnitude* cannot be set. (Blocked-on: a MI-50-safe ingest + a generator choice; see overnight log.)

**Correction 2 — "write-time extraction" must be HYBRID-INDEX, not REPLACEMENT.** The registry's rebuild thesis is right in direction but must carry our own guardrail: extraction-as-REPLACEMENT (deterministic aggregation over extracted facts/events alone) is **CAPPED at ~12–17%** by two independent measurements (atom-oracle atom-alone 16.7%; tonight's LLM-event probe 11.8%). The ceiling-OPENING version is extraction-as-**INDEX into PRESERVED raw**, answered from the hybrid — supported by the atom+raw oracle (60%) and the SOTA-hybrid literature. So the rebuild design is **facts INDEX into raw, never facts REPLACE raw.** We already have hard evidence both FOR the right version (60% oracle) and AGAINST the naive one (17% cap) — an unusually well-constrained rebuild direction.

**Net:** the rebuild thesis (write-time extraction as a hybrid index into preserved raw) is the best-supported architectural direction we have. But the **decision** between (a) fix and (b) rebuild waits on the LME-S baseline to reveal the true gap — that measurement is the gate, not more levers.

## Self-Critique

### What Worked
- Each variant has a distinct voice that matches its assigned message and aesthetic
- The Peter Simmons bio maintains consistent facts (30 years, McAfee 16, SentinelOne 4, Progress Software, eRoom Technology, Senior SE role) while shifting emphasis and tone per variant
- Headlines stay within 8-12 word range as requested
- Subheadlines hit the 20-30 word range
- CTAs are tight (2-4 words) and action-oriented
- Copy is written at roughly grade 8-9 reading level -- short sentences, common words, no jargon
- The target buyer's internal monologue is directly addressed in each variant

### What Could Improve
- The "brutal" variant could push harder on the pain -- it pulls punches slightly in the about section
- The "selector" variant needs a routing mechanism (quiz, cards, etc.) that goes beyond copy -- this is a design/UX dependency
- The "minimal" about section might feel too dry -- the "clean scientific" aesthetic risks losing the human element that makes Peter's story compelling
- All CTAs could benefit from A/B testing alternatives (e.g., "See the Truth" vs "Stop Guessing Now")
- Could include secondary CTA text for each variant

### Cross-Variant Observations
- The strongest emotional hook is in "brutal" -- the direct address to career risk resonates
- "Trust" has the highest enterprise-readiness but may feel corporate for the target audience (small teams, not big companies)
- "Hero" walks the best line between aspiration and accessibility
- "Minimal" is the most information-dense per word, which matches its intent
- "Selector" depends entirely on the UX design -- the copy alone cannot route users effectively
